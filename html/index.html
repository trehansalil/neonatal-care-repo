Deep dive into the specifics of eating, potting, and hygiene for hydronephrosis.

Feeding Strategy: The "Flush"

With hydronephrosis, the goal isn't just nutrition; it's maintaining renal flow. Dehydration causes urine to concentrate, which can lead to crystal formation or stones, and makes bacterial growth easier.

  • ‚úì On Demand + Limits: Feed whenever hungry, but do not let the baby go more than 3-4 hours without feeding, even at night initially.
  • ‚úì No Water: Newborns generally do not need extra water. Breast milk or formula provides the correct solute load. Water can mess up electrolytes.
  • ‚úì Cluster Feeding: This is normal in evenings. It helps build a fluid reserve for the longer sleep stretch.

3

Diaper Changing Protocol

  • Change Immediately: Do not let stool sit. Bacteria from stool can migrate to the urethra (UTI risk).
  • Wipe Direction: strictly Front to Back. Never wipe back towards the urethra.
  • Barrier Cream: Use freely. Healthy skin prevents bacterial colonization.
  • Frequency: Aim to change every 2-3 hours or immediately upon soiling.

Infection Prevention (UTI)

In hydronephrosis, urine may sit in the kidney longer (stasis), making it a breeding ground for bacteria.

Fever > 38¬∞C (100.4¬∞F)

In a hydronephrosis baby, unexpected fever is a UTI until proven otherwise. Go to ER/Doctor.

Cloudy/Smelly Urine

Strong ammonia smell or fishy odor is a red flag. Consult doctor.

Antibiotics (Prophylaxis)

If prescribed (e.g., Amoxicillin), give at the same time daily. Consistency is key to protection.

Bath Time

Avoid strong bubble baths or harsh soaps that can irritate the urethra.
